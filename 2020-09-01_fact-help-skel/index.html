Frequently Asked Questions

Logging in to the FACT Inspection and Accreditation Workshop

Since you are accessing these FAQs from within the workshop, we assume you were able to log in successfully.

  • Can someone else log in for me?
    No, your login information is specific to you and should be kept private. For security purposes, you can only be logged in from 1 location at a time.
  • What equipment and technical specifications are needed to access FACT Inspection and Accreditation Workshop and participate in the workshop?
    If you can access the web, you can participate in the FACT Inspection And Accreditation Workshop! However, to be successful we strongly encourage using a PC or Mac with a keyboard, camera, microphone, and strong internet connection.
    In general, here are the specific requirements:
    • PC, Mac, tablet, or smartphone (PC or Mac is higly recommended)
    • Internet connection (wired is better than wi-fi)
    • Modern web browser. The FACT Inspection And Accreditation Workshop will support the latest versions of Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge. IE11 is not fully supported and you should use a different browser.
    • Please upgrade to the latest version of your chosen web browser. Although our system supports several versions of Chrome and Firefox, we do not support older versions of Edge or Internet Explorer.
    • Please upgrade to the latest version of your chosen web browser. Although our system supports several versions of Chrome and Firefox, we do not support older versions of Edge or Internet Explorer.Check if your browser is up to date here
    • If using Microsoft Edge, please make sure you have the latest version. You can download the latest version of Microsoft Edge here.
    • If you plan to participate in the workshop from a tablet or smartphone, there are certain features that will not be enabled for you, including screen sharing and chat in some meeting rooms.
    • An external or integrated webcam if you are a presenter and wish to share your video during your session.
    • An external or integrated microphone if you wish to have audio during the Lunch with Leaders session.

Best Practices for Meeting Participation (for presenters)

To ensure that your participation in the workshop is as successful as possible, we recommend the following best practices:


Equipment
  • General Requirements
    You will need access to either a PC, Mac, tablet, or smartphone. A PC or Mac is recommended. To participate in the meetings, it is recommended that you have an internal or external camera so that you can be seen on video.
  • Microphones
    Clear audio can make a big difference. Laptop/phone audio can work, but audio through an external microphone (connected by wire or Bluetooth) can be a huge plus. We recommend:
    • Logitech USB Headset H390 with Noise Cancelling Mic
    • Avid Education 1EDU-AE36WH-ITE Headphone with Boom Microphone, Single Plug, White
  • Browser
    The workshop will support the latest versions of Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge. IE11 is not fully supported and you should use a different browser.
    • Internet Connection Tips
      In general, wired connections are better than wireless connections, and wireless connections are better than cellular based connections.
    • While FACT supports tablets and smartphones, we recommend using a PC or Mac as some functions will not work on a tablet or phone.
  • Your Surroundings
    • Find a quiet area for participation. Avoid areas with loud background noise (traffic, wind, loud vents, or appliances).
    • Be one with the light and find the balance! Try to avoid a dark room. Natural room sunlight is good – just do not stand with the light shining right behind you.
    • Consider the acoustics of the room. A small room with dead acoustics will not flatter a voice.
    • Position yourself relatively close to the camera
    • Frame the camera so that your eyes are in the top third of the frame.
    • Use a plain, uncluttered background.
  • Prepare for the Unexpected
    • This is technology, some things *may* go wrong.
    • Have a back-up plan for if someone gets sick, if someone’s internet connection drops, if a laptop crashes, if someone’s audio goes haywire. Can someone quickly take over the presentation?

We are here to help you get the most out of the workshop, so please be in touch! Have a wonderful time!
