FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

LOGGING IN TO ABC

Since you are accessing these FAQ’s from within the ABC virtual meeting, we assume you were able to log in successfully.

  • Can someone else log in for me?
    No, your login information is specific to you and should be kept private. For security purposes, you can only be logged in from one location at a time.
  • What equipment and technical specifications are needed to access ABC and participate in the private meetings?
    If you can access the web, you can participate! However, to be successful we strongly encourage using a PC or Mac with a keyboard, camera, microphone, and strong internet connection.

    In general, here are the specific requirements:
    • PC, Mac, tablet, or smartphone (PC or Mac is highly recommended)
    • Internet connection (wired is better than wi-fi)
    • Modern web browser. ABC thrives in the latest versions of Chrome, Safari and Edge. IE11 is not fully supported and you should use a different browser.
    • Please upgrade to the latest version of your chosen web browser. Although our system supports several versions of Chrome and Firefox, we do not support older versions of Edge or Internet Explorer.
    • If using Microsoft Edge, please make sure you have the latest version. You can download it here.
    • If you plan to participate from a tablet or smartphone, there are certain features that will not be enabled for you, including screen sharing and chat in the private meetings.
  • Can I access the platform before the event begins to confirm my equipment will work?
    Yes, attendees will be given access to the virtual event platform beginning September 2nd. Additionally, on September 2nd and 10th, a test version of the private meetings will be available from 10:00am – 4:00pm Eastern Time. Visit the “MY SCHEDULE” tab to access the meeting on your schedule. In the test meeting, you can test your camera and microphone and explore the features and controls of the private meeting rooms.

    Please note: the Test Meeting room is open to ALL attendees during this time. It is not a private room.
  • How do I sign up for notifications?
    You can sign up for notifications by clicking on the “bell” icon on the bottom left hand corner of the screen. Notifications can be sent to desktop browsers or mobile devices.

SCHEDULED MEETINGS

  • In what time zone are the meetings scheduled?<
    All times displayed on the ABC site are in Eastern Time (ET)
  • Where do I see my confirmed meetings?
    You can see and access your confirmed meetings in the “MY SCHEDULE” tab at the top of the screen
  • How do I access my private meetings?
    Click on the “MY SCHEDULE” tab in the top menu bar. Your meetings will be displayed within the calendar. The calendar will open to the current upcoming date.
  • Why does my schedule include all my company’s meetings – do I need to attend all of them?
    Your schedule will show all meetings that have been scheduled by your company; you only need to attend the meetings you were designated to attend.
  • What features are available in the private meetings?
    • Video and presentation sharing
    • Webcam and audio
    • Share your screen
    • Mute/unmute yourself
    • Chat with all attendees
    • Grid or speaker views
    • Adjust your settings to switch your camera or audio input/output
  • How soon before the scheduled start time can I access the private meeting room?
    Suppliers can access the private meeting room 15 minutes prior to the scheduled meeting start time. Retailers can access the private meeting rooms 5 minutes prior to the start time.
  • Can suppliers do a rehearsal prior to the meeting if presenting?
    Suppliers can access their meeting room 15 minutes prior to the scheduled time to rehearse.
  • Can we continue the meeting past the scheduled stop time?
    There is a 10-minute grace period after the 45-minute meeting. After 10 minutes, the meeting will automatically end, and participants will not be able to re-enter the room.
  • What if I become disconnected from the meeting?
    Return to the “MY SCHEDULE” page and click on the meeting link again. If you are having problems joining the meeting, please click on the “HELP” tab in the top menu bar or on the “Live Chat” icon in the bottom right hand corner of your screen.
  • How do we prepare for the meeting?
    • Log in to the site to make sure your meetings are correct. If anything is missing or incorrect, please contact Marc Thomas at mthomas@fmi.org
    • Read the “Best Practices for Meeting Participation” below
  • What if the private meeting does not recognize my preferred speaker and camera?
    • If you have multiple speakers, microphones, and cameras, you will need to tell the private meeting rooms about your preferences.
    • Click on the “Gear” icon in the lower right corner of the private meeting rooms.
    • Select Devices, then select your preferred speaker, microphone, and camera.
    • Your preferences will be saved on that browser. If you log in from another browser, you may need to select your Device options again.

SPONSORS

  • Where can I find sponsor information?
    Click on the “SPONSORS” tab in the top menu bar to view sponsor information and connect with sponsors.

COMMUNICATING WITH OTHER PARTICIPANTS

  • Where can I see who is attending?
    Click on the "ATTENDEES" tab on the top menu bar. You will see a random selection of participants and can search for other participants by first name, last name, or company. A full, sortable list can also be found on the ABC website.
  • Are the private meetings being recorded?
    No, neither the meeting itself, nor the chat messages are recorded.
  • Can I get a transcript of the chat in the private meeting rooms? No, the chat messages are not recorded.
  • Can I contact or message other attendees?
    Yes, you can search for an attendee by first name, last name, or company under the “ATTENDEES” tab on the top menu bar. Then click on the “Send a Message” button.

TROUBLESHOOTING

  • How do I get help if I am having technical issues? There are several ways to get help if you are having issues with the site.
    1. Click on the “HELP” tab on the top menu bar
    2. Click on the help icon on the bottom right corner or your screen to chat live with a support person
    3. Call (206) 219-1246
    4. Email us abcinfo@fmi.org
    5. Live technical support is available every day of the conference from 8:00am – 5:00pm ET
  • What should I do if...
    • My scheduled meeting is not showing up on my schedule? Contact Marc Thomas at mthomas@fmi.org or email the ABC help desk at abcinfo@fmi.org
    • I cannot access a private meeting? Please click on the “HELP” tab on the top menu bar or click on the help icon on the bottom right of your screen to chat live.
    • I can’t hear/see other attendees in a private meeting or they can’t hear/see me? Please click on the “HELP” tab on the top menu bar or click on the help icon on the bottom right of your screen to chat live.
    • My audio/video is buffering.
      1. Check the speed on your internet connection
      2. Try turning off your camera. A camera can consume a lot of bandwidth
      3. Close all other applications running on your device.
      4. Make sure that your device is as close to the router as possible. If possible, use a hard-wired connection
      5. Click on the “Help” tab on the top menu bar or on the help icon on the bottom right of your screen to chat live.
    • I hear an echo when I talk or from other attendees.
      1. Try muting and then unmuting.
      2. If possible, do not use the built-in microphone and speakers on your laptop. Use an external headset or speaker.

BEST PRACTICES FOR MEETING PARTICIPATION

To ensure that your participation in ABC is as successful as possible, we recommend the following best practices:

  • Equipment
    • General requirements
      • You will need access to either a PC, Mac, tablet, or smartphone. A PC or Mac is recommended. To participate in the meetings, it is recommended that you have an internal or external camera so that you can be seen on video.
    • Microphones
      Clear audio can make a big difference. Laptop/phone audio can work, but audio through an external microphone (connected by wire or Bluetooth) can be a huge plus. We recommend:
      • Logitech USB Headset H390 with Noise Cancelling Mic
      • Avid Education 1EDU-AE36WH-ITE Headphone with Boom Microphone, Single Plug, White
    • Browser
      • ABC will support the latest versions of Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge. IE11 is not fully supported and you should use a different browser.
    • Internet Connection Tips
      • general, wired connections are better than wireless connections, and wireless connections are better than cellular based connections.
    • While ABC supports tablets and smartphones, we recommend using a PC or Mac as some functions will not work on a tablet or phone.
  • Your Surroundings
    • Find a quiet area for participation. Avoid areas with loud background noise (traffic, wind, loud vents, or appliances).
    • Be one with the light and find the balance! Try to avoid a dark room. Natural room sunlight is good – just do not stand
    • with the light shining right behind you.
    • Consider the acoustics of the room. A small room with dead acoustics will not flatter a voice.
    • Position yourself relatively close to the camera
    • Frame the camera so that your eyes are in the top third of the frame.
    • Use a plain, uncluttered background.
  • Screen Sharing
    • Use two monitors: one for the video chat; one for the presentation
    • If sharing full-screen in Powerpoint, you sometimes have to share out a separate window or you’ll see the Presenter’s view
    • Or have another team member run the presentation
    • Practice ahead of your presentation
    • Meeting Participation
      • We encourage participants to turn on their camera in the private meeting rooms so that other participants can see who is talking. But please note that only 16 people can be on video at once in the private meeting rooms.
      • Please mute yourself when not talking to avoid background interruptions (barking dogs, phones ringing, family members talking etc.)
      • Please review the Code of Conduct for online meetings – LINK up to the PDF provided - ML
    • Have a Meeting Plan
      • Since only 12 people at a time can be on video, make a plan with your team to decide who will be on video, who will speak when, who will share the presentation, etc.
      • Consider coordinating your meeting agenda with your trading partner. You can contact them ahead of time via email or phone (if you have their contact information) or you can use the messaging feature in ABC site.
    • Prepare for the Unexpected
      • This is technology, some things *may* go wrong.
      • Have a back-up plan for if someone gets sick, if someone’s internet connection drops, if a laptop crashes, if someone’s audio goes haywire. Can someone quickly take over the presentation?

Have a Terrific ABC! Thank you for participating!
